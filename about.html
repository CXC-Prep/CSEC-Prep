About

Purpose:

One of the most effective ways to revise is to do past papers, however, it’s quite daunting to open a past paper booklet and start doing past papers just like that. Thus this website tries to make it easier for others to revise by allowing students to target specific topics as opposed to doing an entire past paper at a time. This is done by students choosing a topic that they would like to study and getting all the past paper questions that cover that topic.

How to use?

To use this website it is necessary to have access to past papers as the content for the questions aren’t made available here since that would conflict with CXC’s copyright policy.


To find questions of a specific topic simply: go to the subject’s page, select the topic from the filter by checking its checkbox and unchecking the checkboxes of the topics that you don’t want to see (this can be done easily by selecting ‘none’ and then the topic of choice) and then scroll below to see the questions that deal with that specific topic.

Background:

One of the most important child rights is the right to an education. As a result, this website serves to make education more accessible by making it easier to study on one’s own. During the COVID-19 pandemic, one of the biggest problems that I faced in preparing for the CSEC examinations was how difficult it was to start revising. CSEC Prep was created to hopefully make it easier for students to study by making revision with past papers more approachable.


The inspiration for this project came from my school teachers who would give us a list of past paper questions that dealt with the topic that we recently covered in class to help us revise. The motivation for completing this project, however, came from my involvement in the Child Rights Ambassador Programme (a programme of the Gender and Child Affairs division of the Office of The Prime Minister of Trinidad and Tobago in collaboration with UNICEF).
